Who are Academic REPs?

Academic Representatives are volunteers recruited by the Studentsâ€™ Union who work with their Department to co-create student-led change based on student feedback. Academic Reps volunteer until the end of the academic year, with the option to renew their role every year.

We are on the mission

We constantly trying to make The University Of Sheffiled a better place to study at. We proudly serve as a bridge of communication between students and staff, in matters such as teaching, examinations, departmental policies and student issues.

What do Academic REPs do?

They work roughly an hour per week in term time and their job mostly includes these responsibilities:

  • Listening to and learning the views of students in your Department, even when itâ€™s feedback you might not agree with;
  • Bring feedback to Student Staff Committees (or SSCs) in your Department and Academic Forum in the SU to lead change on wider-ranging issues, such as welfare and wellbeing in the curriculum, equality and liberation issues, and student rights;
  • Working with Department staff to resolve day-to-day issues, ensuring that students are receiving good education on a well-run course;
  • Work with staff to share your feedback and success, letting students know that their feedback has been heard.

Faculty REPs

Faculty Representatives are volunteers recruited by the Studentsâ€™ Union who take on leadership roles in the Academic Rep system to promote student-led change. Faculty Reps volunteer until the end of the academic year, with the option to renew their role every year.

Faculty REPs - Description

Faculty REPs tend to work for approximately 2 hours per week during the term-time. Their responsibilities differ from the position mentioned above. To be more precise, they have following responsibilities:

  • Undertaking the usual responsibilities of the Academic Rep role, or ensuring that any missed representation work is done is covered by another student in your cohort;
  • Leading SSCs as a Student Chair or a Student Secretary, facilitating conversation between Academic Reps and improving student ownership of the meeting;
  • Sharing feedback from your SSC at Faculty Student Staff Committees, where you can work on Faculty-level student voice projects;
  • Working with Reps across Departments to identify areas for collaboration and shared practice;
  • Providing student voice in University spaces, such as Departmental Education Committees, Faculty Education Committee, or EDI-related spaces in your Department.
